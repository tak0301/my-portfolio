Transnational Security

Instructional Design

Transnational Security is a course offered annually through The George Washington Universityâ€™s Elliot School of International Affairs. This particular version of the course was designed for a 14-week fully online format.

As lead instructional designer on this project, I worked closely with the course instructor to revamp existing content in order to more effectively engage students in an online environment. Our main focus was on recrafting course assignments and producing high quality media. I introduced, for instance, the use of the tool Voicethread into the course in order to stimulate multimedia-rich discussion among the students. I also worked closely with the instructor to produce a series of chunked up video lectures that include motion graphics and animated text.

ABOUT ME

An educator for nearly 10 years, I entered the online education space in 2010 while working for an international nonprofit organization. Over the past 5 years, I have worked with content experts in the higher education and nonprofit sectors to develop and produce online education that pushes boundaries and strives to enhance learning for all.
