Public Administration

Instructional Design, Case Study Simulation

Foundations in Public Administration is a course offered through The George Washington University’s Trachtenberg School of Public Policy and Public Administration. This particular version of the course was developed for a fully online 6-week format.

As lead instructional designer on this course, I worked closely with the instructor at all stages of course development. One main area of focus was on the design and development of a series of case study simulations that would be used in the course to challenge students to think critically and make real-life public administration-related decisions. The case study simulations were videos designed to include interactive decision-point questions, as well as professionally acted out scenes to illustrate a case’s critical dilemma.

ABOUT ME

An educator for nearly 10 years, I entered the online education space in 2010 while working for an international nonprofit organization. Over the past 5 years, I have worked with content experts in the higher education and nonprofit sectors to develop and produce online education that pushes boundaries and strives to enhance learning for all.
